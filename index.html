About Me

Research Profile

I am a computational and materials chemist with an MPhil in Chemistry from Hazara University, Pakistan. My research bridges experimental nanomaterial synthesis with first-principles computational modeling to design and characterize advanced functional materials.

My work focuses on Density Functional Theory (DFT) investigations of perovskites, chalcogenides, MAX phases, Zintl compounds, and half-Heusler materials. I explore their structural, electronic, optical, elastic, thermodynamic, and thermoelectric properties for applications in optoelectronics, spintronics, and renewable energy.

I have also contributed to green synthesis of metal and semiconductor nanoparticles, photocatalytic degradation of organic dyes, and adsorptive removal of heavy metals from aqueous solutions.

Focus Areas

Research Interests

Computational Materials Science

First-principles DFT calculations using WIEN2K and Materials Studio for structural, electronic, and optical property predictions.

Nanomaterials & Photocatalysis

Green synthesis of metal and semiconductor nanoparticles for photocatalytic degradation of toxic organic dyes and environmental remediation.

Optoelectronic Materials

Investigation of perovskites, chalcogenides, and MAX phases for solar cells, LEDs, and UV optoelectronic applications.

Spintronics & Magnetism

Study of magnetic ordering, half-metallicity, and spin-dependent transport in double perovskites and Zintl compounds.

Thermoelectric Materials

Engineering band gaps and thermoelectric properties in advanced materials for waste heat recovery and energy conversion.

Medicinal & Green Chemistry

Biogenic synthesis of nanoparticles, heavy metal extraction, and electrochemical applications in environmental and health sciences.
